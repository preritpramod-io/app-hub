02

Thesis and market

The gap. The last two decades reshaped the cost of reaching orbit. The next decade belongs to whoever can close the loop and bring value back down to the ground with precision and at scale. That return path is the open gap in the space economy, and it is the one Multiscale Space Systems is built to fill.

The model. The company sells the recoverability layer, the subsystem that lets a third party spacecraft come home under control, rather than a full capsule service. The return capability becomes a software defined service that other vehicles build on.

Where controlled return matters first

  • In space manufacturing recovery. Bringing materials and products made in orbit back to a chosen site intact.
  • Recoverable orbital experimentation. Flying an experiment and returning the hardware and the data.
  • Hypersonic flight test. A controllable, recoverable platform through the hypersonic regime.
  • Defense return platforms. Responsive, precise return of assets for national security users.
03

What we own

the full reentry stack as IP

The core technology is a morphing deployable aeroshell, an electromechanically actuated structure that serves as both the vehicle's thermal protection and its aerodynamic control surface at the same time. By reshaping the body of the vehicle in flight, the descent is steered through the hypersonic regime using onboard guidance and control rather than chemical thrusters, which removes the propellant, the plumbing, and much of the cost and failure surface that have kept controlled reentry expensive and rare.

Multiscale Space Systems is an integrated systems company. Where most of the field specializes in one slice of the descent, the company treats reentry as a single coupled problem spanning structures, aerothermodynamics, actuation, power, and real-time control, and it owns that full stack as intellectual property. The aeroshell is the enabler. The coupled control of the deforming body and the fault tolerant electromechanical, power, and energy system that the control depends on are the invention.

04

Intellectual property strategy

IP first

The current strategy is IP first. Prerit generates a patent portfolio covering the full reentry vehicle system stack while Shardul runs the raise and the partnership outreach. The inventive locus is the specific control strategy and how petal angle commands translate, through closed loop position control, into commanded vehicle dynamics, together with the electromechanical, power, and fault tolerant systems that make that control real.

Filing discipline works hardware side up and files broad genus level independent claims first, then narrows. Three inventions are on record with anchors, and the portfolio is mapped against a prior art wall built from the closest deployable and control adjacent work.

Valuation

subsection · placeholder

Valuation at pre-seed and pre-flight is driven by the strength of the team, the size of the return gap, and the defensibility of the IP, far more than by current revenue, which is zero by design at this stage.

Value drivers

  • Ownership of the full reentry stack as IP, not a single component.
  • An integrated systems moat that is hard to assemble piecemeal.
  • A large, structurally open market, controlled return, with few credible entrants.
  • A founder with a deep safety critical control and power electronics track record.

Reference points

  • Accelerator instruments seen so far are standard, for example a small common stock check plus an uncapped most favored nation SAFE, which set a floor rather than a true priced valuation.
  • A priced number waits for the first program partner and the entity conversion.
